Ecuador

Beauty, multiculturalism and community; are three words that describe Ecuador and that’s why in this year’s performance for this country, we are trying to portray the diversity and traditions Ecuador has had from its independence until now.

Beginning with the Amazon part, “Pachamama", translated as the mother earth, is normally adored among the native communities through soft dance movements. We then move to the mountains, where the colourful and fast paced “San Juanito” brings history and traditions alive. Now for modern times, we’ve decided to pick salsa as a reference to the afro ecuadorian contribution as a renovation to traditional movements. Salsa is danced all over Latin America, especially in the coasts of the countries where the music, mixed with movement, create a non-stopping fiesta. Finally, but not less importantly, contemporary contributions like reggaeton and latin-pop have been chosen to exemplify the Ecuadorian and Latin-American contribution to today's music world, which not only portrays the culture of Hispanoamérica, but also brings Latinx people together, where music connects them independently of where they are.
