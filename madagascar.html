Madagascar

“Aleo very tsikalakalam-bola toy izay very tsikalakalam-pihavanana”, is a malagasy proverb that translates to ‘it is better to lose money than the peace and love that are among us’ . One thing we have found is that you cannot talk about Madagascar without talking about peace as Madagascar is a country that values a lot peace in the community, because of this, it was very important for us to fully represent reunion in our dance. Not only because it is this year’s theme but also because one of the key factors of peace in a society is through the togetherness of a community and as covid restrictions globally are able to slowly subdue the malagasy people are able to restore that sense of peace in their community.

The dance that we'll perform will demonstrate how the Malagasy people support and enjoy bringing everyone together in one area. Our performance will demonstrate how happy we are when we are all together and united. The performance will constitute of a Malagasy traditional dance performed at any gathering or celebration, whether familial, political, or festive events. this dance is usually the first dance that everyone will dance at such events.

Our performance is then followed by a dance that will be carried out by a song that directly means "life". This song highlights almost all the advantages Malagasy people find of being in this world. Such as being with the family, having the possibility to love life and to enjoy it in the present moment. This song also thanks those who still allow us to live in this world, especially God and parents and acts as a reminder to the people of Madagascar to give their thanks for this life and ultimately their reunion after being apart for so long.
